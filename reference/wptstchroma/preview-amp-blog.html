Tips for Preparing Your Child for Preschool

By Sarah Johnson · December 30, 2024 · 4 min read

Starting preschool is a big milestone for both children and parents. With the right preparation, you can help your little one feel confident and excited about this new adventure.

1. Start with Small Separations

Before the first day, practice short separations. Leave your child with a trusted caregiver for brief periods to help them understand that you'll always come back.

2. Establish a Routine

Children thrive on predictability. Start implementing a morning routine that mirrors what preschool mornings will look like:

  • Wake up at the same time each day
  • Get dressed independently (with help as needed)
  • Eat a nutritious breakfast
  • Practice saying goodbye cheerfully

3. Read Books About Preschool

Stories about starting school can help your child visualize what to expect. Some favorites include "The Kissing Hand" and "Llama Llama Misses Mama."

"Children who feel prepared are more likely to separate easily and engage quickly with their new environment." — Dr. Emily Chen, Child Development Specialist

4. Practice Self-Help Skills

Help your child feel more independent by practicing:

  • Using the bathroom independently
  • Washing hands thoroughly
  • Putting on shoes and jacket
  • Opening lunch containers

5. Visit the School Together

If possible, schedule a visit to the preschool before the first day. Let your child explore the classroom, meet the teachers, and see where things are located.

Regulatory Briefing

1. The Section 125 Umbrella

The mechanism operates under the strict guidelines of Internal Revenue Code Section 125 (Cafeteria Plans). This enables W-2 employees to reallocate portions of their wages on a pre-tax basis into qualified benefit accounts. Because these deductions occur *before* taxation, neither the employee nor the employer pays FICA (Social Security & Medicare) on that allocated income.

Key Statutory Provisions

§ 125
Cafeteria Plans Construct Employees receive a choice between cash and qualified, non-taxable benefits. Pre-tax premium payments establish the required reduction in taxable gross payroll.
§ 105
Amounts Received Under Accident and Health Plans Guarantees that amounts paid by an employer to reimburse an employee for medical care (like Manhattan Life or Revive components) are not included in gross income.
§ 213
Medical, Dental, & Healthcare Expenses Deals with the definition of preventative care and the deductibility of associated costs out-of-pocket, solidifying the pre-tax nature of the health policy.
